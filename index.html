A DLT Market Mechanism for better Benefit Transfers






International Aid is in crisis. Not because of a lack of supply, but because aid is simply not reaching beneficiaries. Lack of incentives to complete the benefit transfer, and non-transparent processes that encourage graft are the two main reasons for this.

The DLT concept of 'Non Fungible Tokens' or NFTs can be used to address this.

Represent Benefits as NFTs

NFTs are unique - only one instance can ever exist at a given time. Rather than allocating hard-to-track dollars to large groups of people, create unique, non-transferable benefits for each individual. Once transferred, the BNFT is 'burned', ensuring no duplicate transfers are possible.

Incentivize 'Agents' to deliver the BNFT

The funded agency model is not very efficient at delivering benefits to beneficiaries. BNFT creates market-based incentives for NGOs, entrepreneurs and other stakeholders to ensure the transfer of benefits. This creates a market for benefit transfers.

Learn More

Cryptographically verify that the transfer took place

Cryptographic verification ensures that an immutable record of all transactions exist that can never be altered once committed. This greatly reduces the scope for graft as the details of the transaction are publicly verifiable, even by third parties.

Learn More




All BNFTs thus created, transferred and verified will now have an individual trail, publicly and auditably recorded on the blockchain. Third-party oversight is just a matter of utilizing a block explorer to match facts on the chain with acts on the ground. If they do not match, it is public record which agent and verifier were involved. As NFTs, duplicates cannot exist, and they cannot be applied twice. Each benefit is unique and individual.

Bundle millions of BNFTs into 'Aid Instruments' that donors can buy on the BNFT exchange

Now that the individual benefits can be created on the blockchain, we can bundle millions of such targeted benefits into 'Aid Instruments' that can be purchased by donor agencies in a transparent exchange-like environment. A donor can now directly invest in, say, '100 thousand scholarships for graduating girls in Uganda', rather than an abstract program that aims to deliver the same goal.



BNFT is not a panacea, and may not be applicable to all cases. But many situations could benefit from a market-economics approach to benefit delivery, rather than the difficult-to-monitor stakeholder-agency approach.

About Us

BNFT was born after years of work on building Monitoring & Evaluation systems for international projects across India. We have observed that the report-and-milestone-based funding model often fails to provide the right incentives to achieve better performance in transfers, and it is also fairly easy for bad actors to game the system. The unique properties of trustless auditability and game mechanics for incentives in Distributed Ledger Technologies led us to the creation of BNFT.
